## Strategic Summary

### Where AEGIS Wins

1. **Quality gate depth** — 7 specialized VERIFY/FEEDBACK skills vs 2 in BMAD
2. **Infinite loop** — Feedback automatically feeds next cycle; BMAD is linear
3. **Marketing pipeline** — Full research→content→visuals→delivery automation (BMAD has nothing)
4. **Security-first** — Dedicated security-audit skill with OWASP, secrets, infra scanning
5. **Adversarial rigor** — Formal BREAK framework with kill scenarios, not just concept-level
6. **Platform flexibility** — Works on any Claude environment, not just IDEs
7. **Thai-native** — Bilingual triggers and responses for Thai teams
8. **DevOps depth** — git-workflow + tech-debt-tracker + sprint-tracker + retrospective + course-correction all owned by Forge
9. **API docs lifecycle** — Auto-generation, drift detection, changelog — BMAD has nothing

### Where BMAD Wins

1. **Module ecosystem** — 5+ official modules including Game Dev and Test Architect (TEA)
2. **Community & momentum** — 35k+ stars, active Discord, YouTube tutorials, 111 contributors
3. **Open source** — MIT license, community-driven development

## Final Verdict

**AEGIS surpasses BMAD in depth and automation.** BMAD surpasses AEGIS in community and ecosystem breadth. For a team building enterprise software with a focus on security, quality, and Thai-market products, AEGIS is the stronger choice. For a solo developer who wants maximum community support and pre-built modules, BMAD is excellent.

They are **complementary, not competing** — a team could use BMAD for project scaffolding and AEGIS for quality gates.
